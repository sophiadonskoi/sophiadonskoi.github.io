About Me

I’m a master’s candidate focused on climate communication at the University of Florida. I graduated from UCLA, summa cum laude, with a B.A. in Public Policy and Environmental Systems and Society. I served as the Communications Lead for Jaden Smith’s environmental nonprofit, 501CTHREE.org from 2020-2023, and prior to that, was the Assistant General Manager of UCLA Radio, a student-run nonprofit, where I produced several environmental radio programs. I have been a journalist from my elementary school’s morning announcements through college radio production. I am experienced in public interest communications, social impact, writing, multimedia storytelling, audio production, story development, and scientific and technical communication. I am a first-generation American who speaks fluent Portuguese, Russian, and English. In my free time, I love drinking matcha, spending time with loved ones (that includes my cat, Framboesa), and kayaking. In 2021-2023, I was recognized by the Aspen Institute as one of 100 Future Climate Leaders, and am also a Rachel Carson Council Fellow and a Florida Climate Institute Fellow. I have written speeches for Jaden Smith and Al Gore, and produced content for Complex, Apple TV, YouTube, and NowThis. In the future, I hope to broaden my impact as a professor of Environmental Communications, teaching others how to effectively tell empowering stories about our environment.
